The model
01
The thesis

Physical AI is being designed for Western homes, smart cities, and enterprise facilities. The billions of people in Nigeria, Kenya, Ghana, Rwanda, and the rest of the Global Majority are absent from those assumptions.

02
Our approach

Build practical systems that work inside the infrastructure people actually have: intermittent power, unstable internet, mid-tier hardware, messaging apps in place of broadband. Ship locally first. Expand outward.

03
The horizon

Each deployment, with consent, produces real-world data from environments almost entirely missing from current training sets: the infrastructure layer for physical AI in emerging markets.

02 Why now
“AI is likely to close the technology divide,” offering developing countries a chance to narrow long-standing gaps through its accessibility and abundance.
— Jensen Huang · CEO, NVIDIA

We agree, with a caveat.

Every prior wave reached Africa assuming infrastructure that wasn't there. AI is the first we can design for those constraints from the start, but the divide only closes if someone builds the layer underneath.

03 First product

Gateman-AI

Chat with your cameras on WhatsApp.

A small box on your premises. AI in the cloud. Alerts in WhatsApp. Built for the way Lagos actually works.

A WhatsApp-native security and accessibility agent for homes and small businesses. It connects a camera in your environment to a chat you already use, with no app to install and no app-store subscription. A small on-premise box runs alongside any RTSP camera; AI inference is hybrid by default, choosing local or cloud based on the operator's connectivity.

  • →Instant notification with a photo and an AI scene description the moment something happens
  • →Ask "who's at the gate?" and get a live photo back in seconds
  • →Ask "what happened in the last hour?" and the model reads back the event log
  • →Works with the RTSP IP cameras you already have, with no rip-and-replace
  • →The box on your premises keeps capturing and logging through power and internet outages
