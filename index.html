Lagos, Nigeria — Building Physical AI

Physical AI for Africa.
Built from Lagos.

The Global Majority deserves physical AI that works within its actual infrastructure — intermittent power, unstable internet, WhatsApp-shaped connectivity, and hardware budgets closer to $50 than $700.

See what we're building ↓
The problem

Physical AI is designed for Western infrastructure. It assumes broadband, flagship hardware, and cloud subscriptions that don't work in most of the world.

Our approach

Build on infrastructure that already exists — WhatsApp, low-cost hardware, local AI inference. Start in Lagos. Expand across Africa and the Global Majority.

The opportunity

8.8M Nigerian households are adopting smart security by 2029. No affordable, locally-built AI layer exists yet. That gap is where we build.

First product
Gateman-AI
Open Source

A WhatsApp-native security and accessibility agent for homes and small businesses. Connects a camera at your entrance to WhatsApp — no custom app, no cloud subscription, no per-message fees. Runs on a $35 Raspberry Pi or any existing RTSP camera. All AI inference stays local. Nothing leaves your network.

  • Instant WhatsApp notification with photo and AI scene description
  • Ask "who's at the gate?" and get a live photo back
  • Ask "what happened in the last hour?" and Gemma reads the event log
  • Works with existing Hikvision, Dahua, or any RTSP camera
  • Designed for unreliable power, unstable internet, tight hardware budgets

"The gate was locked for over a year. We had no way to know who was coming."

A bakery manager near Yabatech Polytechnic in Lagos described the daily fear of not knowing who was outside during a period of repeated gun violence in the area. Armed forces guard the school. Civilians around it have nothing.

The Raspberry Pi powering Gateman-AI came back from Austin, Texas in my luggage. Two years at Eagle Eye Networks working with enterprise security camera infrastructure showed me exactly what it could do — and exactly who it could not reach.

Back in Lagos, that gap was impossible to ignore.
