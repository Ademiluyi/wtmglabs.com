The model
01
The thesis

Physical AI is being designed for Western homes, smart cities, and enterprise facilities. The billions of people in Nigeria, Kenya, Ghana, Rwanda, and the rest of the Global Majority are absent from those assumptions.

02
Our approach

Build practical systems that work inside the infrastructure people actually have: intermittent power, unstable internet, mid-tier hardware, messaging apps in place of broadband. Ship locally first. Expand outward.

03
The horizon

Each deployment, with consent, produces real-world data from environments almost entirely missing from current training sets: the infrastructure layer for physical AI in emerging markets.

04 First product

Gateman-AI

AI that watches your gate, accessible through WhatsApp.

A small box on-site. Events, not footage. Answers on WhatsApp. Built for the way Lagos actually works.

A WhatsApp-native AI that watches your gate. A small box on-site plugs into the cameras you already have and turns a passive CCTV feed into an attentive assistant — instead of hours of footage nobody watches, you get events, described in plain language and answerable like a conversation.

  • →WhatsApp-native. No new app to learn — alerts and answers arrive where you already are
  • →On-premise, resilient. The box keeps capturing through power and network cuts, buffering locally and syncing when the connection returns
  • →Events, not footage. Each event is text plus one key frame — ~38 KB for a day of activity, not gigabytes of video. Cheap to store, instant to search
  • →Conversational, grounded. Ask in plain language and it answers from what it actually saw, never invented
  • →Standing watches. Tell it once — “alert me if anyone lingers more than two minutes” — and it watches, day and night
